Agapanthus

Agapanthus

Agapanthus africanus

ag-uh-PAN-thus af-ruh-KAN-us

View flower details

Description

Tall, elegant 16 - 30" (40 - 75 cm) stalks bear 3 - 6" (8 - 16 cm) globe-shaped clusters of smaller ¾ - 1½" (2 - 4 cm) tubular florets, which open slowly across 3 - 4 days. Flowers are waxy, sturdy and without fragrance.

Special care

Extremely ethylene sensitive; should be treated with an anti-ethylene product prior to standard processing. Then hold at room temperature for 1 - 2 hours (up to 4 if stems are obviously stressed, with bent necks or wilting) before storing in in a 39 - 41°F (4 - 5°C) cooler at 80 - 90% humidity. Temperatures below this range may cause the flowers to turn reddish.

Fun Facts:

  • Herbalists use agapanthus flower essence to promote a sense of abundance and aspiring to one’s true potential
  • Linear, versatile and dramatic in traditional arrangements.
  • Florets may be wired and taped individually for corsages.
  • A member of the Amaryllidaceae family, related to garlic, snowdrops and narcissus.
